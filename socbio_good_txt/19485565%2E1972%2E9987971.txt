Population/Family Planning Programs

Seminar-Workshops in Management and
Planning of Population/Family Planning
Programs: This series began in 1970 to fill
the need for short-term management-oriented training for administrators of family
planning programs in developing countries.
Six seven-week seminar-workshops have
been successfully completed with excellent
response and evaluation from the participants. The next seminar-workshops are
scheduled for June 19-August 4 and October 2-November 17, 1972.

The seminar-workshops introduce new
ways of thinking about Population/Family
Planning to help participants develop in
sights into their individual country’s problems. The workshops also encourage the
interchange and examination of experience
among many countries and cultures, and

provide information and tools useful in pro-_

gram planning and management.

Seminar sessions are held at the Governmental Affairs Institute, Washington, D.C.
Enrollment is limited to 25 participants.
AID-sponsored participants need pay no
tuition costs. For further information, contact Director, Management Services, Governmental Affairs Institute, 1776 Massachusetts Ave. N.W., Washington, D.C.
20036.

Maxillo-facial Communicative Disorders Seminar

The Lancaster Cleft Palate Clinic will
conduct its 16th Seminar, “Habilitation-Rehabilitation of Maxillo-facial Communicative Disorders,” October 2-6, 1972.

Integration of multidisciplinary diagnostic and treatment practices will be presented pertaining to total patient care.
Participants will observe plastic surgery,
the construction of prosthetic appliances
and speech retraining procedures. Clinic and
research staff will present lectures and demonstrate their data collection process re
garding our longitudinal study program.
Coordination of inter- and intradisciplinary
services, parent or patient genetic counseling, and psycho-social behavior will be discussed.

Emphasis will be placed upon minimal
effects to growth and development of midfacial complex through conservative surgical and dental management. For additional
information and application, write to
Robert T. Millard, Program Director, 24
North Lime Street, Lancaster, Pa. 17602.
