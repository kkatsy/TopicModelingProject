Opening for a Sociobiologist, University of Wisconsin-Madison

The University of Wisconsin-Madison is seeking a Ph.D. Assistant Professor for the Institute for Environmental Studies and the Department of Anthropology. Candidates should have a strong background in biology and the social sciences with evidence of broad research interests. The successful applicant will take responsibility for a core course on "The Social Science Perspective in Environmental Studies." There will be an opportunity for research and teaching of higher-level courses in an appropriate interest area. The appointment is for a two-year period with the

possibility of an additional appointment. Send curriculum vitae with letter de-
scribing research interests and experience to: Chairman, Search and Screen Committee, 70 Science Hall, SSO N. Park St., University of Wisconsin-Madison, Madison, WI S37O6. Include the names of three persons from whom references may be solicited. Applications close December 1, 1977. The University of Wisconsin-Madison is an equal opportunity employer. Women and minorities are encouraged to apply.
