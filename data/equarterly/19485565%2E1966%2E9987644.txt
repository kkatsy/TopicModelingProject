MINUTES OF THE 1965 ANNUAL MEETING OF THE AMERICAN EUGENICS SOCIETY

Downloaded by [University of Michigan] at 13:28 08 December 2015

The Annual Meeting of the American Eugenics Society was held on Tuesday, November 9, at 12:00. Notice of the meeting was sent to all members. The Meeting was held in Room 3101, 230 Park Avenue, New York, New York.
Mr. Frederick Osborn acted as the Chairman of the Meeting. There were present in person or by proxy 188 members.
The Chairman called the meeting to order and requested Miss Sally Johns to act as secretary of the Meeting. The Minutes of last year's Meeting were read and approved. The Nominating Committee (Frederick Osborn, Harry Shapiro, and Gordon Allen) presented the following slate of Directors to be reelected for the class of 1968:
C. Nash Herndon Richard H. Post Frank Lorimer Sheldon C. Reed Frederick Osborn Harry Shapiro
Bruce Wallace
There were no other nominations made from the floor. On motion, carried unanimously, the Secretary was instructed to cast one ballot for the proposed slate.
The Treasurer's Report was approved and accepted, and the Auditor's Statement was ordered to be made a part of the minutes of the Meeting. The Treasurer presented a cash statement as of

October 31, 1965, which was approved and ordered attached to the minutes. The Finance Committee reported the value of stocks held in endowment fund of the Society to a total of something over $36,000.
This year the Society is publishing a Five-YearReport: 1960-1964,which will be sent to the members of the Society.
The Secretary reported that the arrangements with Technipress, Inc., of Washington, D. C, for publication of the Eugenics Quarterly will continue. There is an increase of 25% in the number of pages printed in each issue. Dr. Richard H. Osborne will continue as Acting Editor.
Following last year's Workshop of Population Genetics and Demography, the Society is sponsoring a Second Princeton Conference, to be held November 19 and 20 at the Princeton Inn. This Conference will include psychologists and an anthropologist as well as geneticists and demographers. A summary report of the Conference will appear in the Quarterly in 1966.
With no further business to discuss, the Chairman adjourned the meeting.
Respectfully submitted,
FREDERICK OSBORN
Secretary
